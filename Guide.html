  1. Read each assigned chapter from the textbook to gain an in depth knowledge about the information covered in class
  2. Schedule 1-3 hours to focus on cengage SAM Excel assignments, the weekend before it is due so you can ask any assignment questions in office hours
  3. Record class discussions about case studies to listen to them again before quizzes or exams
  4. Follow tech journalism outposts such as: TechCrunch, Forbes, CNAT, TechRadar, and The Verge, to stay updated on industry trends to see how Porter’s Five Forces are reflected in real life

ABOUT THE CLASS

The MIS 301 course at UT Austin is an undergraduate course for Mccombs BBA students with an interest in management information systems or students pursuing a degree in MIS. This class bridges information technology with modern business to teach proficiency in skills such as: Excel, SQL, HTML and Survey making. Furthermore, throughout this course, students explore the functions of databases, data communication, mobile telecommunications, business process management, and business intelligence in the 21st century. The real value of this subject emits in the real world as competitive organizations use innovative IT to transform the political, cultural and social landscape of our fast paced society.
